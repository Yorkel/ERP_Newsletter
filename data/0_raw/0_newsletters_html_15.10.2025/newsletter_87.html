Updates from the Programme

 

Technology and Classroom Practice: what needs to change

12 November 2025, 5:30pm–7:00 pm (Online)

In the next instalment of the What matters in education?  panel discussion series, John Gordon, David Lundie and Rebecca Eynon will discuss findings from their ERP projects about technology’s place in the secondary school classroom. They will look at why current practice needs to change and how that could happen. Together with Dr Sangeet Bhullar, Executive Director of WISE KIDS and Martin Oliver, UCL Institute of Education, they will reflect on how to use technology to enhance rather than diminish pedagogic practice at a time of escalating social inequalities.

Political environment and key organisations

  

OECD - Results from The OECD Teaching and Learning International Survey (TALIS)  2024

TALIS is the world’s largest survey of teachers and principals. In 2024, 280 000 educators from 55 education systems shared insights into their working conditions, professional development and the realities of the modern classroom. In this edition, Results from TALIS 2024, teachers reveal if and how they use artificial intelligence, why they choose teaching, and if they wish to remain in the profession.
