Political environment and key organisations

 

HEPI blog - However: The Curriculum and Assessment review

Chris Husbands takes a look at the new Interim Report of the Curriculum and Assessment Review. He concludes that the report is "thorough, technical and thoughtful" but leaves many questions unanswered. "To simplify: attainment gaps open early; they are not closed by the curriculum and assessment system, and one of the few graphs in the report (p 18) suggests that they are widening, leaving behind a large minority of learners who struggle to access a qualifications system which is not working for them. "

More

 

SchoolsWeek - ‘Evolution’ may not be quick enough to meet our challenges  

Tony Breslin argues that the language of school improvement needs to change: "The existing language of school improvement frames current levels of pupil absenteeism as a crisis of school attendance; a new language would frame it as an issue of educational engagement." He questions whether the current government's approach to system reform goes far enough
