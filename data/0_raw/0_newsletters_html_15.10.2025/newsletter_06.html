Lack of catch-up plan widens regional results gap
Sir Kevan Collins, the Government's former education recovery tsar, has warned that the Government's failure to invest in a full catch-up plan for schools post-Covid has contributed to a widening gap in exam results between pupils in the north and south. Collins argued that increasing rates of absenteeism and a "piecemeal" approach to catching up had fed through to A-level and GCSE results. Collins said: "This was the biggest disruption in education since the second world war and there was no moment of saying: 'Right, we are going to grab this and address it.'" He added that ministers made a "huge mistake" and "let children down". He urged Rishi Sunak to revisit the problem, stating: "It's not too late to support the groups coming through. It's not only about money, it's about money alongside signals and supporting schools. It's about making clear this is something we nationally prioritise and I don't think that message has got across."
The Sunday Times   

 

School absences rise by over 10%

School absences have risen by more than 10% in a year due to the ongoing impact of lockdown anxiety, according to the Children's Commissioner. The number of children missing school has increased by 200,000 within the last 12 months, reaching 1.8m for the academic year 2022/23. The rise is attributed to rising mental health issues among children, particularly anxiety, as well as unmet needs of children with special educational needs. Dame Rachel de Souza stressed the importance of getting children back into school and learning, urging all sectors, including health, police, and social services, to prioritise attendance.
