Full story - https://schoolsweek.co.uk/chatgpt-keegan-launches-call-for-evidence-on-ai-in-schools/

 

DfE

 

Revealed: the experts advising ministers on teacher training reforms review

The Department for Education has appointed an “external steering group” to review both the initial teacher training and early career frameworks, first launched in 2019.

The group is made up of seven experts who are “closely familiar” with both reforms, as well as the “underpinning evidence”.

They will help “shape the work of the review, scrutinising, supporting and challenging our thinking”, the DfE said.

Full story - https://schoolsweek.co.uk/revealed-the-experts-advising-ministers-on-teacher-training-reforms-review/

 

Reject fewer teacher applicants, DfE tells trainers

Susan Acland-Hood, the DfE’s permanent secretary, told providers a 7 per cent jump in applicants this year had not led to an equivalent rise in offers for courses. 

Full story - https://schoolsweek.co.uk/reject-fewer-teacher-applicants-dfe-tells-trainers/

 

Ofqual and DfE studying ‘feasibility’ of ‘fully digital’ exams

A long read from Nuffield funded research project ‘Advancing Leadership Development in Early Years Education via Digitally Mediated Professional Learning’

In brief, the report finds:

  1. there is an essential need for connection and reflection in DMLD (digitally mediated leadership development), where leaders have the opportunity to learn through the narratives and experiences of one another.
  2. There is a desire to move towards blended delivery models that centre evaluation.
  3. the urgent need to carve out space for professional learning.

 

Full report - https://www.nuffieldfoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/Project-Report-Advancing-Ear-Years-Leadership-Development.pdf
