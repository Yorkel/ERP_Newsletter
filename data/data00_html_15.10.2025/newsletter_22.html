The DfE have today published their Areas of Research Interest (ARI).  

Department for Education: areas of research interest - GOV.UK (www.gov.uk)

 The ARI set out DfE research priorities and the key areas they hope to workon  with the research communities. The headline topics are schools, skills, early years and family support, vulnerable children, and artificial intelligence (AI) and technology. The ambition is to ensure that DfE continues to be informed by the best available evidence that can contribute to its priority strategy, policy and delivery decisions. 

They  are planning a series of engagement events "to facilitate collaboration between research experts, external organisations, DfE analysts and policy colleagues on each of the topic areas"  - watch out for details

Political landscape & key organisations

 

 IfG - Whitehall Monitor 2024

 Sets out the Institute’s view on long-term reform priorities for the civil service. Areas for improvement include capacity and structures for longer-term, open, cross-cutting and imaginative policy making; for capitalising on the digital and AI revolution; and for improving resilience in Covid-type national crises.  Includes a section on: “Too much policy neglects evidence and remains closed to outside input, despite real progress on government’s data capabilities.”

Government school attendance campaign.

 MPs Robin Walker and Steve Brine, the chairs of the Education Committee and the Health and Social Care Committee, have jointly written to Health Secretary Victoria Atkins and Education Secretary Gillian Keegan to call for a public awareness campaign to help parents manage their children's illnesses, address the "crisis" of school absenteeism, and relieve pressure on GPs.
