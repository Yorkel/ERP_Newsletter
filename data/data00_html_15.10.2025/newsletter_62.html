The Conversation - Long hours and high pressure are putting significant strain on teachers’ personal and professional lives

A study  commissioned by the Educational Institute of Scotland (EIS) found that nearly a quarter of Scottish teachers’ lesson time was spent on low-level and serious behaviour interruptions. They spent time dealing with distressed behaviour and incidents of verbal and physical aggression, settling the class and working with pupils on individual plans to help them engage better with school.
