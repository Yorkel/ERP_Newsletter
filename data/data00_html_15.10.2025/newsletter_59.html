Enhancing teacher agency with technology: Creating an ecological model through a place-based study of teaching and learning PI: John Gordon

 

Problematising education and digital technology webinar

 Tuesday 18th February 2025 at 15:30- 17:30 (Online)

Join the UEA team for an exciting webinar as part of a global event convened by Neil Selwyn, Professor of Digital Technology, Society and Education  looking at how and why teachers use technology (EdTech) to build a new model for understanding teachers’ agency with technology in context.  

Some of the questions to be explored: 

What are the pressing issues, concerns, tensions and problems that surround EdTech in our locality?

What social harms are we seeing associated with digital technology and education in our locality?   

What does the political economy of EdTech look like in our region?

What grounds for hope are there?

Embedding children’s participation rights in pedagogical practice in lower primary classrooms in Wales  PI: Sarah Chicken

 

Children’s Participation in Schools project Second annual symposium

Thursday 6th March from 3.30 – 5pm
This event focuses upon the second phase of our three-year ESRC funded project exploring the following research questions:

• How do primary teachers in Wales conceptualise and support young children’s participative rights and what are perceived enablers and barriers to practice?

• How are young children's participative rights in Welsh primary schools understood and experienced by young children themselves?

You will hear reflections from the research team, some of our teacher participants and the artist- consultant who facilitate the dialogic sessions.
Join us to find out more and discuss the implications and recommendations of this phase of the research.
