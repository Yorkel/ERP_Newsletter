Updates from the projects

 

Decentring the ‘resilient teacher’: exploring interactions between individuals and their social ecologies PI: Steph Ainsworth

 

The Manchester Met team have been busy sharing their research on teacher resilience at the Comparative and International Education Society Conference (Chicago) and ResilienceCon (Nashville). The key finding related to each paper are summarised below.

 

 

In summary, the papers found that the predictors of teacher resilience vary by outcome, with individual factors linked to wellbeing and contextual factors related to job satisfaction and burnout. Leadership practices and colleague support influence resilience through social-ecological systems, while schools use 'ecology mapping' to identify local changes, though systemic constraints limit their efforts; teachers also report increased challenges with parental communication post-COVID-19, feeling unsupported and overwhelmed by expanded roles and constant digital communication.

What matters in education?

 
SMF - We used to have it all: is there a trade-off between wellbeing and attainment in schools? 

By 2022 the UK had seen the largest fall in pupils’ sense of belonging in school of any country in the OECD’s Programme for International Student Assessment. Through a literature review and new analysis of OECD data, this briefing paper argues that attainment would not be compromised by placing more emphasis on wellbeing, as some have suggested; and sets out the steps government should take to address this, arguing that this would lessen the current high levels of pupil absence in England.
